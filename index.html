The problem
Growth keeps stalling.
Innovation stays siloed.

Companies invest in innovation initiatives but rarely see lasting results. Ideas get generated but don't get built. Progress stalls when there's no structure to move from insight to action. The challenge isn't creativity — it's the absence of an operating model that makes innovation part of how the organization works every day.

The Innovation Operating Model addresses the four dimensions where organizations most often stall: strategy, culture, leadership, and structure.

Our approach
Three types of innovation.
One operating system.

Not all innovation is the same. The IOM helps organizations identify and balance three distinct types — so they can build new capabilities while strengthening their core.

Incremental

Improving what already exists — optimizing products, services, and processes to deliver more value with less friction.

Evolutionary

Adapting to change by developing adjacent capabilities, entering new markets, or creating new offerings for current customers.

Revolutionary

Transformative innovation that creates entirely new categories, disrupts existing markets, or redefines how your organization operates.

The process
Five phases. One human-centered flow.

The 5D framework guides teams through a complete innovation cycle — from understanding the challenge to delivering a tested solution. Each phase builds on the last, ensuring the right problem is solved the right way.

D1

Devise

Align on the challenge worth solving. Teams define the strategic intent, clarify the innovation type, and establish the boundaries and success criteria for the work ahead.

D2

Discover

Deeply understand the people at the center of the challenge. Research, observe, and listen to surface the real needs, behaviors, and motivations driving the opportunity.

D3

Define

Synthesize what was learned into a clear, actionable problem statement. This phase ensures the team is solving the right problem — grounded in human insight, not assumption.

D4

Design

Generate, develop, and refine ideas. Teams move from concepts to prototypes, testing their thinking quickly and iterating toward a solution that works in the real world.

D5

Deploy

Bring the solution to life. Define the rollout plan, create the feedback loops, and ensure the innovation lands with the people it was built for.

Whether you're running a half-day sprint or a multi-week program, the toolkit scales to fit your team's size, speed, and challenge.

The Story of Growth Toolkit
The Hero's Journey
as a growth map.

The Hero's Journey — Joseph Campbell's universal story of growth — isn't just a narrative framework. It's a map for anyone navigating meaningful change. The Story of Growth Toolkit applies this arc directly to personal and professional growth.

Whether you're stepping into a new role, navigating uncertainty, or looking to contribute at a higher level, the five stages of the Hero's Journey give you a language for where you are — and a path forward.

The Call

A moment of disruption, opportunity, or invitation that asks you to grow beyond your current identity. It can feel exciting or terrifying — often both.

The Threshold

The decision to step forward, even without certainty. This is where commitment begins and the familiar starts to fall away.

The Trials

The challenges, failures, and breakthroughs that build capability and character. This is where most of the real growth happens.

The Revelation

A moment of clarity — about who you are, what you're capable of, and what you're meant to bring. The core gift of the journey.

The Return

Bringing what you've learned back to your team and organization. The hero's gift isn't just personal — it's shared.

Archetypes
Know your role
on the team.

Every person has a natural way of showing up — especially under pressure. The Story of Growth Toolkit uses 12 Jungian archetypes to help individuals identify their default patterns, leverage their natural strengths, and recognize which archetype they need to grow into next.

The Hero

Driven to prove their worth through courage and action. Shows up with determination, resilience, and a willingness to take on challenges others avoid.

The Revolutionary

Compelled to challenge the status quo and break what isn't working. Brings disruptive energy, honest truth-telling, and a vision for what's possible.

The Explorer

Restlessly seeking new experiences, ideas, and possibilities. Brings fresh perspectives, an openness to the unknown, and a drive to discover what's next.

The Creator

Driven to build something meaningful and lasting. Brings imagination, originality, and the discipline to turn ideas into tangible reality.

The Ruler

Motivated to create order, stability, and a clear path forward. Brings vision, decisiveness, and the ability to build systems that endure.

The Caregiver

Compelled to support, nurture, and protect the people around them. Brings empathy, loyalty, and a deep commitment to the wellbeing of the team.

The Sage

Driven to find truth and share knowledge. Brings analytical depth, wisdom, and a commitment to making decisions grounded in evidence.

The Idealist

Motivated by a deep belief that things can be better. Brings optimism, moral clarity, and the energy to inspire others toward a higher vision.

The Realist

Grounded in what's practical and what actually works. Brings pragmatism, reliability, and the ability to make the best of any situation.

The Jester

Uses humor, play, and lightness to unlock creativity and connection. Brings energy, fun, and the ability to help teams break free from rigid thinking.

The Lover

Motivated by connection, beauty, and meaning. Brings passion, enthusiasm, and the ability to make people feel seen, valued, and inspired.

The Magician

Driven to transform reality by understanding what lies beneath the surface. Brings intuition, synthesis, and the ability to make the seemingly impossible possible.

The problem
Most growth decisions are made
without the customer in the room.

Most organizations make their most important growth decisions — what to build, what to change, what to stop — with limited visibility into what their customers actually experience. Research happens in bursts. Surveys arrive late. Insights sit in decks no one reads.

The result is a persistent gap between what organizations believe is true about their customers and what is actually true. Converci closes that gap.

The assumption trap

When teams don't have access to real customer input, they fill the gap with internal assumptions. Those assumptions accumulate until they become the foundation for major decisions — without anyone questioning them.

The feedback lag

Traditional research moves slowly. By the time insights are gathered, synthesized, and shared, the window for action has often passed. Converci delivers ongoing, real-time visibility.

The insight gap

Data without meaning is just noise. Converci doesn't just collect customer input — it processes it through the 4 i's framework: Information, Insights, Implications, and Impact.

How it works
The 4 i's of data:
from input to impact.

Converci replaces episodic research with a continuous, community-driven insights engine. The 4 i's framework turns raw customer input into strategic direction your team can actually act on.

01

Information

Gather raw data directly from your customer community — behaviors, reactions, and unfiltered perspective on what's working and what's not.

02

Insights

Distill the patterns that matter. Surface the deeper truths hidden in customer responses — the recurring themes, the surprises, and the signals worth acting on.

03

Implications

Identify what the insights mean for the business. Translate customer reality into strategic direction — clarifying where to invest, what to change, and what to stop.

04

Impact

Generate measurable growth. Apply the implications to product decisions, experience design, and brand strategy — and close the loop by measuring what changes.
